What is peptic ulcer?

A peptic ulcer is a lesion in the mucosal membrane of the upper GI tract( stomach, duodenum) Causes: The common causes of peptic ulcers:

  • Bacterial infection with Helicobacter pylori is the cause of 90% of peptic ulcers
  • Use of nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drugs (prostaglandin inhibitors)
  • blood typeA (common in people with gastric ulcers) and blood typeO (common in those with duodenal ulcers) , genetic factors, exposure to irritants, stress and anxiety, normal aging

Pathogenesis:

According to cause

  • In a peptic ulcer due to H. pylori -H. pylori releases a toxin that destroys the stomach's mucus coat, reducing the epithelium's resistance to acid digestion and causing gastritis and ulcer disease
  • .In a peptic ulcer due to nonsteroidol anti-inflommotory drugs(NSAIDS): -These drugs used mainly as analgesic and anti-inflammatory but one of the side effects is that they inhibit COX1 that produce prostaglandins in stomach which has a role in protection of the stomach wall during digestion.

    Inhibition of prostaglandins causes:

  • Inhibition of the insoluble mucus thot oct as protective barrier
  • Inhibition of NCOs which neutralize acidity of the stomach wall during digestion
  • Decrease blood flow during digestion causing ulcer

Complications of peptic ulcer:

  • Erosion of the mucosa: This can cause gastrointestinal hemorrhage, which can progress to hypovolemic shock
  • Obstruction of the pylorus due to inflammation and edema

Signs and symptoms:

  • Loss of weight and appetite
  • Abdominal pain which increased by eating, heartburn, and indigestion
  • The feeling of abdominal fullness
  • Anemia is caused by blood loss so gives Pale skin.
